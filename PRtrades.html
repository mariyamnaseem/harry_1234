Federal Skilled Trade (FST)


The Federal Skilled Trades (FST) is a pathway for skilled trades workers to immigrate to Canada as permanent residents. It's one of the programs managed under the Express Entry system, which is Canada's primary immigration management system for skilled workers..

Educational Requirement


The FSTP generally requires candidates to have at least a high school diploma or its equivalent. Specific educational requirements may vary depending on the skilled trade the applicant is applying under..


For some trades, candidates may need to have completed a specific apprenticeship program or obtained a certificate of qualification. These requirements ensure that applicants have the necessary skills and knowledge to perform the job duties associated with their chosen trade.


In addition to meeting educational requirements, candidates must demonstrate proficiency in English or French, as language skills are essential for communication and integration into Canadian society. Language proficiency is typically assessed through standardized tests such as IELTS for English or TEF for French.


While education is important, the primary focus of the FSTP is on assessing candidates based on their work experience, skills, and ability to contribute to the Canadian labor market in a skilled trade capacity.

Language Requirement For (CRS)


Candidates must demonstrate proficiency in English or French, Canada's two official languages. Proficiency is assessed in four key areas: speaking, listening, reading, and writing.


To prove language proficiency, candidates typically need to take standardized language tests approved by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). For English, the International English Language Testing System (IELTS) General Training or the Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program (CELPIP) are accepted. For French, the Test d'évaluation de français (TEF) is commonly used.


Candidates must achieve minimum scores on these tests to meet the language requirement. The minimum scores can vary depending on the immigration program and the level of language proficiency required.


Language proficiency plays a significant role in the Express Entry system, which manages the FSTP along with other immigration programs. Candidates receive points based on their language test results, which contribute to their Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score. A higher CRS score increases their chances of receiving an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residency. Language Instruction Programs: In some cases, candidates may be required to attend language instruction programs to improve their language skills before or after arriving in Canada. These programs aim to help newcomers integrate into Canadian society and the workforce more effectively. Overall, meeting the language requirement is essential for FSTP candidates to demonstrate their ability to communicate effectively in either English or French, which is crucial for their success in the Canadian labor market and society.
