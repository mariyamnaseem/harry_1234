Requirement

It is likely that you will need to take the IELTS Academic test if your program is taught in English. The minimal score necessary may change based on the school and program you're applying to. Undergraduate programs normally require an overall band score of 6.0 or 6.5, although graduate programs may demand a higher score, typically in the range of 6.5 to 7.5.


You must have been accepted by a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) in Canada.You will receive a letter of acceptance from the university, which you will need to apply for a visa.


You need a valid passport for the duration of your stay in Canada. It's advisable to renew your passport if it's close to expiration.


You have to prove that you have the funds to pay for your living expenses, tuition, and round-trip transportation. Bank statements, evidence of a Canadian bank account, or a sponsor's letter of financial support can all be used to support this.


You may need to provide a police clearance certificate to prove that you have no criminal record.


You may need to provide biometric information (fingerprints and a photo) as part of your application.


You'll need to fill out the appropriate application form for a study permit and pay the application fee.

Visa availability

Canada's immigration policy is generally open to international students who wish to study in the country. The Canadian government actively promotes international education and values the contributions of international students to its education system and society.

Canada does not have a quota system for study permits like some other countries. As long as applicants meet the eligibility criteria and provide the required documentation, they can typically obtain a study permit.

To apply for a study permit, international students need to submit their applications online or through a Canadian visa application center (VAC) in their home country or country of residence. The application process involves providing biometric information, proof of acceptance to a designated learning institution (DLI), proof of financial support, and meeting health and security requirements.

Processing times for study permit applications can vary depending on the volume of applications received and other factors. Generally, applicants are encouraged to apply well in advance of their intended start date to allow for sufficient processing time.
